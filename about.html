About me

Hi there! My name is Daniel Hillerström, and I am a passionate computer science student. I saw the first rays of sunlight in 1990, and I grew up in lovely nature of Virklund in Denmark. Currently, I live in Edinburgh, Scotland, where I study computer science at the University of Edinburgh.

My primary interests in computer science are semantics and type systems for programming languages, and compilers. Moreover, I have a sweet spot for mathematics, especially, mathematical analysis. I am very keen on fixed points theorems and computations (definition: A point \(x\) is called a fixed point of a function \(f\) if \( f(x) = x \)).

Current work

At the moment I am working on my master’s dissertation Handlers for Algebraic Effects in Links. My aim is to implement first-class handlers for algebraic effects in the functional web-programming Links.
